-/ About Me /-

I recently finished my master's degree in Computer Science at Nation Taiwan Normal University (NTNU). During my two-year of study, I had a strong interest in the field of computer graphics, and when I joined the Visual Data Analysis (ViDA) Laboratory, I was a teaching assistant in computer graphics course and a member of the 2022 Computer Graphics Workshop (CGW) staff.

-/ My Research /-

My primary research goal is to address the issue of data occlusion in 3D space. In order to provide non-expert users with simple learning and exploration of complex 3D volumes, we have developed a 3D volume interactive exploration system for use in medical, education, and museum exhibitions, among other applications. During the previous two-years of research, I've also read serveral papers on occlusion management , volume deformation, and labeling of volume structure.
